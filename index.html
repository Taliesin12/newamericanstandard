Philosophy

We want to serve society and improve the world by improving the lives of individuals.

Education needs more than just an update to fit the current era we all live in. Educational reform isn’t a new idea, and yet major issues persist. We need to stop adding branches to a decaying tree and start with fresh roots—start with a new way to measure learning. The letter grade system prioritizes batch methods of teaching and rewards cramming information. Rather, we need a new standard of communication that values the individual mind and the retention of knowledge and skills. By achieving this standard not only will we accurately track a student’s progress but we will also empower students, teachers, and employers.
This education system will not be designed like a computer. Because of it’s adaptability it is designed to function more like the actual brain.
